<li><b>3-Coloring in time O(1.3446<sup><i>n</i></sup>): a no-MIS algorithm</b>.<br>
<a href="https://www.cis.temple.edu/~beigel/">R. Beigel</a>
and D. Eppstein.<br>
<a href="ftp://ftp.eccc.uni-trier.de/pub/eccc/reports/1995/TR95-033/index.html">
Tech. Rep. 95-033</a>, Electronic Coll. Computational Complexity, 1995.<br>
<i>36th IEEE Symp. Foundations of Comp. Sci.</i>, 1995, pp. 444&ndash;453.<br>
<a href="https://www.cis.temple.edu/~beigel/FESNP/">DIMACS
Worksh. Faster Exact Solutions for NP-Hard Problems</a>, 2000.
<p>
Speeds up 3-coloring by solving a harder problem:
constraint satisfaction in which each variable can take on
one of three values and each constraint forbids a pair of
variable assignments.  The detailed solution involves
several long hairy case analyses.
Similar methods apply also to 3-list-coloring,
3-edge-coloring, and 3-SAT.  The 3-SAT algorithm is fixed-parameter tractible in that it is polynomial time when the number of 3-variable clauses is O(log n).
Merged into
<a href="p-3color2.html">3-coloring in time O(1.3289^n)</a>
for the journal version.

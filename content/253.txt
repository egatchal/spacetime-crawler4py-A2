<li><b>The centroid of points with approximate weights</b>.<br>
<a href="https://www.parc.xerox.com/csl/members/bern/">M. Bern</a>,
D. Eppstein, 
<a href="https://robotics.stanford.edu/users/guibas/bio.html">L. J. Guibas</a>,
J. Hershberger, 
<a href="https://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~suri/">S. Suri</a>, and 
<a href="https://www.wwnet.net/~janc/">J. Wolter</a>.<br>
<a href="BerEppGui-ESA-95.ps.gz"><i>3rd Eur. Symp. Algorithms,</i>
Corfu, 1995</a>.<br>
Springer, <i>Lecture Notes in
Comp. Sci.</i> 979, 1995, pp. 460&ndash;472.
<p>
Given a set of points with weights that are not known precisely,
but are known to fall within some range, considers the possible
weighted centroids arising from different choices of weights
in each range.  The combinatorics of this problem are closely
connected with those of
<a href="geom-zono.html">zonotopes</a>.

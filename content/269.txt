          <h2>Erik B. Sudderth, <small>Statistical Computation &amp; Perception</small></h2>
          <p>I am a Professor of <a href="http://www.cs.uci.edu/">Computer Science</a> and <a href="https://www.stat.uci.edu/">Statistics</a>, and Chancellor's Fellow, at the <a href="http://www.uci.edu/">University of California, Irvine</a>.  My <a href="group/">Learning, Inference, &amp; Vision Group</a> develops statistical methods for scalable machine learning, with applications in artificial intelligence, computer vision, and the natural and social sciences.  My research affiliations at UC Irvine include:</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Director of the <a href="https://cml.ics.uci.edu/">UCI Center for Machine Learning and Intelligent Systems</a>.  See our <a href="https://cml.ics.uci.edu/aiml/">seminar series</a>.</li>
            <li>Director of the <a href="https://www.ics.uci.edu/hpi/">HPI Research Center in Machine Learning and Data Science at UC Irvine</a>.</li>
            <li><a href="https://vision.ics.uci.edu/">UCI Computational Vision Group</a>.</li>
            <li><a href="https://sites.uci.edu/acoi/">UCI Algorithms, Combinatorics, and Optimization Center</a>.  See their <a href="https://acoi.ics.uci.edu/seminar-series/">seminar series</a>.</li>
            <li><a href="https://create.ics.uci.edu/">UCI Steckler Center for Responsible, Ethical, and Accessible Technology (CREATE)</a>.</li>
            <li><a href="https://ucinoyce.org/">UC Irvine Initiative in AI, Law, &amp; Society</a>.</li>
            <li><a href="https://www.imbs.uci.edu/">UCI Institute for Mathematical Behavioral Sciences</a>.</li>
            <li><a href="http://datascience.uci.edu/">UCI Data Science Initiative</a>.  Also see the <a href="http://www.stat.uci.edu/seminar-series/">statistics seminar series</a>.</li>
          </ul>

          <p>For a tutorial introduction to probabilistic modeling and approximate inference, see the <a href="papers/sudderthPhD.pdf">background chapter of my doctoral thesis</a>, advised by Professors <a href="http://ssg.mit.edu/%7Ewillsky/">Alan Willsky</a> and <a href="http://people.csail.mit.edu/billf/">William Freeman</a> at <a href="http://www.eecs.mit.edu/">MIT EECS</a>.  My postdoctoral research at <a href="http://www.eecs.berkeley.edu/">Berkeley EECS</a>, advised by Professors <a href="http://www.cs.berkeley.edu/~jordan/">Michael Jordan</a> and <a href="http://www.cs.berkeley.edu/~russell/">Stuart Russell</a>, focused on Bayesian nonparametric models (see my <a href="bnpCVPR12/">CVPR tutorial</a>).</p>
          <p>For more information: <a href="bio/">bio</a> &middot; <a href="sudderthCV.pdf">curriculum vit&aelig;</a> &middot; <a href="projects/">research projects &amp; code</a> &middot; <a href="papers/">publications &amp; lectures</a></p>

          <h3>Research Highlights</h3>
          <ul>
            <li>Work on <a href="papers/icml22ai4scienceworkshop.pdf">inference for soil biogeochemical models</a>, an important framework for quantifying the impact of rising global surface temperatures, appears at the <a href="http://ai4science.net/icml22/">ICML 2022 AI for Science Workshop</a>.</li>
            <li>We advance the <a href="papers/neurips21fairnessConstraints.pdf">scalability and stability of fair machine learning methods</a> in work at <a href="https://neurips.cc/Conferences/2021/Schedule?showEvent=28024">NeurIPS 2021</a>.</li>
            <li>The <a href="http://roseyu.com/time-series-workshop/">ICML 2021 Time Series Workshop</a> best poster award goes to our work on <a href="papers/icml21timeseriesworkshop.pdf">prediction constraints for semi-supervised classification with Hidden Markov models</a>.</li>
            <li>Work on <a href="papers/icml21msng.pdf">better black-box variational inference for probabilistic programs</a> appears at <a href="https://icml.cc/Conferences/2021">ICML 2021</a>.  This work supported in part by a <a href="https://research.fb.com/announcing-the-winners-of-the-probability-and-programming-research-awards/">Facebook Probability and Programming research award</a>.</li>
            <li>Our work on <a href="https://research.dshin.org/iccv19/multi-layer-depth/">3D scene reconstruction with multi-layer depth and epipolar transformers</a> appears at <a href="http://iccv2019.thecvf.com/">ICCV 2019</a>.  Previously at the CVPR <a href="https://scene-understanding.com/">3D Scene Understanding</a> and <a href="https://sumochallenge.org/2019-sumo-workshop.html">SUMO Challenge</a> workshops.</li>
            <li>Our cascaded 3D detection framework, which integrates geometric and contextual cues for robust scene understanding from RGB-D images, is summarized by a 2020 <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.04725">paper</a> appearing in <a href="https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8737746">IEEE PAMI</a>.</li>
            <li>An <a href="https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=1816365">NSF Robust Intelligence Award</a> with <a href="https://www.ics.uci.edu/~ihler/">Alex Ihler</a> supports work on new particle-based algorithms for inference and learning with continuous graphical models.  I gave a <a href="slides/socalML17diverseMP.pdf">talk</a> at the <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/socalml17/">2017 SoCal Machine Learning Symposium</a> about our earlier <a href="papers/icml15dpmp.pdf">diverse particle max-product</a> algorithm, which gives state-of-the-art predictions of continuous protein side-chain conformations.  <a href="https://www2.cs.arizona.edu/~pachecoj/">Code available</a>.</li>
            <li>At <a href="http://www.aistats.org/aistats2018/schedule.html">AISTATS 2018</a>, our framework for <a href="papers/aistats18semipc.pdf">prediction-constrained training of probabilistic models</a> leads to improved semi-supervised learning of topic models, with applications to the analysis of documents and electronic health records.  This work received the <a href="https://socalnlp.github.io/symp18/">SoCal NLP Symposium best paper award</a>.</li>
            <li>An <a href="http://cs.brown.edu/news/2014/04/01/erik-sudderth-wins-nsf-career-award/">NSF CAREER Award</a> supports our open source toolbox <a href="https://bnpy.readthedocs.io/">BNPy: Bayesian Nonparametric clustering for Python</a>.  BNPy implements scalable, stochastic and memoized variational inference algorithms for a diverse range of Bayesian nonparametric models.</li>
            <li>Work with <a href="https://www.braingate.org/">BrainGate</a> on <a href="papers/nips17mssmBCI.pdf">multiscale semi-Markov dynamics for improved brain-computer interfaces</a> appeared at <a href="https://nips.cc/">NIPS 2017</a>.  A <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1PCVnr4B8Xc">supplemental video</a> demonstrates accurate, interactive control of a computer cursor by a clinical trial participant with tetraplegia.</li>
            <li>The <a href="https://bayesian.org/project/mitchell-prize/">2014 ISBA Mitchell Prize for Bayesian analysis of an important applied problem</a> goes to our <a href="http://bssa.geoscienceworld.org/content/103/2A/709.abstract">NET-VISA system for global seismic monitoring</a>, learned from data provided by the <a href="http://www.ctbto.org/">comprehensive nuclear-test-ban treaty organization (CTBTO)</a>.  For details see the <a href="https://news.brown.edu/articles/2016/01/testban">Brown University news article</a>.</li>
            <li>Weiss &amp; Pearl <a href="http://www.cs.huji.ac.il/~yweiss/1010TPWeiss.pdf">introduce</a> our review article on <a href="papers/cacm10nbp.pdf">Nonparametric Belief Propagation</a> for the <a href="http://cacm.acm.org/magazines/2010/10">CACM</a>.</li>
          </ul>

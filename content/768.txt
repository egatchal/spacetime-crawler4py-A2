					I am Associate Professor of <A href='https://www.informatics.uci.edu' target='_blank'>Informatics</A> in the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences</A> at the University of California, Irvine.<P>
						
					My research interests lie at the intersection of human&ndash;computer interaction (HCI), computer supported cooperative work (CSCW), and health informatics.<P>
						
					I received my Bachelor of Medicine degree (MBBS) from the <A href='https://www.cmu.edu.cn/en/' target='_blank'>China Medical University</A>, and PhD from the <A href='https://drexel.edu/cci/academics/information-science-department/' target='_blank'>iSchool at Drexel University</A>.<P>

					<DIV id='R' style='display:none'>
					Inspired by my own experience and training, I am interested in the interplay bewteen information, people, organization, and technology in healthcare. <P>
					I study how different stakeholders in healthcare access, document, share, and make sense of complex health data and information in their personal, professional, and social contexts. I am particularly interested in how these activities are mediated, communicated, coordinated and supported through technlogical tools from the sociotechnical perspective.<P>

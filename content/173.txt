<h1 align=center style='text-align:center'><span style='mso-fareast-font-family:

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";
color:black'>1. Course Outline</span></b><span style='mso-fareast-font-family:
"Times New Roman"'><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";
color:black'>VR headsets have been a very popular device for creating new
experiences. In this course we will <span class=GramE>provide an introduction
to</span> all the aspects of virtual reality --- hardware, software, human
perception, and evaluation. The course syllabus includes the following.</span><span
style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in;

</span></span></span><![endif]><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;
mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";color:black'>Introduction and history<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in;
mso-list:l9 level1 lfo8;vertical-align:baseline'><![if !supportLists]><span
style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:
Arial;color:black'><span style='mso-list:Ignore'>b.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span></span><![endif]><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;
mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";color:black'>Geometry of Virtual
World<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in;
mso-list:l9 level1 lfo8;vertical-align:baseline'><![if !supportLists]><span
style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:

</span></span></span><![endif]><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in;

</span></span></span><![endif]><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;
mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";color:black'>VR Hardware - lenses,
cameras, displays, controllers<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in;
mso-list:l9 level1 lfo8;vertical-align:baseline'><![if !supportLists]><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";
color:black'>2. What this course will not cover</span></b><span
style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";
color:black'>This course will not cover any augmented reality (AR) technology.
However, this course will be a good precursor to any AR course. This course
will not teach how to build the VR hardware, however building a simple VR
headset can be an excellent project for a subsequent project course.&nbsp;</span><span
style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";
color:black'>3. Interaction with Other Visual Computing courses</span></b><span
style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";
color:black'>Geometry and rendering of virtual worlds are the topics in this
course that overlaps with CS 112 (Computer Graphics). Although familiarity with
illumination, transformation and animation is important for VR, a <span
class=GramE>10 week</span> course on VR cannot afford to delve in its depths.
Therefore, all the assignments for this course will be done in Unity that
shields the students from implementing all these elements of the graphics
pipeline. However, CS 112 solely focuses on these topics giving students a
detailed tour of the graphics pipeline, associated programming of vertex and
fragment shaders, deep mathematical details of setting up viewpoints, scenes,
illumination, transformations and animations. Interested students can follow up
CS 118 with CS 112 to learn the aforementioned topics in detail. Students who
have taken CS 112 prior to CS 118 will refresh their memories when we cover
geometry and rendering of VR worlds for 2 weeks and will additionally learn how
to use this knowledge in a VR application.</span><span style='mso-fareast-font-family:
"Times New Roman"'><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";
color:black'>CS 118 will not overlap with any other visual computing course
like CS 111, CS 113, CS 114, CS 116 and CS 117. However, it will supplement
these courses creating a strong visual computing concentration, offering
students more options of courses to select from.&nbsp;</span><span
style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:11.0pt;
font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman";

style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><o:p></o:p></span></p>

the camera location.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

learn how to make their VR application compatible to run on mobile VR.
Designing a full experience by the middle of the course helps to keep students
interested to explore more.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'><br

 <li class=MsoNormal style='color:black;text-align:justify;mso-list:l6 level1 lfo10;

     11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;mso-fareast-font-family:"Times New Roman"'>Assignment
     3: </span></b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;

     video. This is an extremely popular application in virtual tourism and

     4: </span></b><span style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif;

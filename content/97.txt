<li><b>On the NP-completeness of cryptarithms</b>.<br>
D. Eppstein.<br>
<a href="https://portal.acm.org/citation.cfm?id=24662">
<i>SIGACT News</i> 18 (3): 38&ndash;40, 1987</a>.
<p>
A cryptarithm
(also known as an 
<a href="https://users.aol.com/s6sj7gt/mikealp.htm">alphametic</a>)
is a puzzle in which the digits of a mathematical formula
(typically addition of two large numbers) are replaced by letters;
the goal is to determine which letter stands for which digits.
If arithmetic is done in a polynomially large radix, the problem
becomes NP-complete.
<p>(<a href="Epp-SN-87.pdf">Full paper</a>)

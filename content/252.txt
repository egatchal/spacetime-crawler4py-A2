<ul>
<li><b>Deterministic sampling and range counting in geometric data streams</b>.<br>
<a href="https://www.ics.uci.edu/~bagchi/">A. Bagchi</a>,
<a href="https://masters.cs.uchicago.edu/page/amitabh-chaudhary-phd">A. Chaudhary</a>,
D. Eppstein, and
<a href="https://www.ics.uci.edu/~goodrich/">M. T. Goodrich</a>.<br>
<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/cs.CG/0307027">arXiv:cs.CG/0307027</a>.<br>
<i>20th ACM Symp. Comp. Geom.,</i> Brooklyn, 2004, pp. 144&ndash;151.<br>
<a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/1240233.1240239"><i>ACM Trans. Algorithms</i> 3(2):A16, 2007</a>.
<p>
We describe an efficient streaming-model construction of epsilon-nets
and epsilon-approximations, and use it to find deterministic streaming-model
approximation algorithms for iceberg range queries and for various
robust statistics problems.
<p>
<li><b>The effect of faults on network expansion.</b><br>
<a href="https://www.ics.uci.edu/~bagchi/">A. Bagchi</a>,
<a href="https://www.cs.jhu.edu/~ankur/">A. Bhargava</a>,
<a href="https://masters.cs.uchicago.edu/page/amitabh-chaudhary-phd">A. Chaudhary</a>,
D. Eppstein, and
<a href="https://www.cs.jhu.edu/~scheideler/">C. Scheideler</a>.<br>
<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/cs.DC/0404029">arXiv:cs.DC/0404029</a>.<br>
<a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/1007912.1007960"><i>16th ACM
Symp. Parallelism in Algorithms and Architectures</i>,
Barcelona, 2004, pp. 286&ndash;293</a>.<br>
<a href="https://doi.org/10.1007/s00224-006-1349-0"><i>Theory of
Computing Systems</i> 39 (6): 903&ndash;928, 2006</a>.
<p>
Studies the resilience of distributed computation networks against
adversarial and random fault models; shows that, in both models,
certain networks can withstand constant fault probabilities and
still contain a large subnetwork with similar expansion to the original.

<li><b>Optimal M&ouml;bius transformations for information visualization
and meshing</b>.<br>
<a href="https://www.parc.xerox.com/csl/members/bern/">M. Bern</a>
and D. Eppstein.<br>
<a href="https://arXiv.org/abs/cs.CG/0101006">arXiv:cs.CG/0101006</a>.<br>
<a href="BerEpp-WADS-01-omt.pdf"><i>7th Worksh. Algorithms and Data
Structures,</i> Providence, Rhode
Island, 2001</a>.<br>
Springer, <i>Lecture Notes in
Comp. Sci.</i> 2125, 2001, pp. 14&ndash;25.
<p>
We give linear-time
<a href="geom-lp.html">quasiconvex programming</a>
algorithms for finding a M&ouml;bius transformation of a
<a href="geom-circle.html">set of spheres</a>
in a unit ball or on the
surface of a unit sphere that maximizes the minimum size of a
transformed sphere. We can also use similar methods to maximize the
minimum distance among a set of pairs of input points. We apply these
results to vertex separation and symmetry display in spherical
<a href="gdraw.html">graph drawing</a>, viewpoint selection in
<a href="geom-hyperbolic.html">hyperbolic</a> browsing, and element size
control in conformal structured
<a href="geom-tri.html">mesh generation</a>.

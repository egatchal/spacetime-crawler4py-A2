<li><b>Planar Lombardi drawings for subcubic graphs</b>.<br>
D. Eppstein.<br>
<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1206.6142">arXiv:1206.6142</a>.<br>
<i>20th Int. Symp. Graph Drawing</i>, Redmond, Washington, 2012.<br>
<a href="https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-642-36763-2_12">Springer, <i>Lecture Notes in Comp. Sci.</i> 7704, 2013, pp. 126&ndash;137</a>.<br>
<p>
We show that every planar graph of maximum degree three has a planar
Lombardi drawing and that some but not all 4-regular planar graphs have
planar Lombardi drawings.
The proof idea combines circle packings with a form of
M&ouml;bius-invariant power diagram for circles, defined
using three-dimensional hyperbolic geometry.
<p>
For the journal version, see "<a href="p-lombardi-soap.html">A
M&ouml;bius-invariant power diagram and its applications to soap
bubbles and planar lombardi drawing.</a>".
